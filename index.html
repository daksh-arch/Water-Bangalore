Bangalore's Hidden Water Economy

Where does the city’s water come from — and where does it go? This data-driven story traces every drop through supply, use, treatment, and reuse in 2021.

Why This Matters

Bangalore’s water crisis isn’t about scarcity — it’s about flow. The city depends heavily on distant rivers, unregulated borewells, and fragile infrastructure. This story maps the city’s water metabolism to reveal where interventions are most needed.

What the Data Reveals

  • 1. Domestic use dominates: Over 70% of all water ends up in homes — yet much of it goes untreated.
  • 2. Leaky pipes, hidden recharge: Infrastructure losses contribute more to aquifer recharge than rainwater harvesting.
  • 3. Reuse is growing, but small: Only 30% of treated wastewater is reused, mostly by industry and landscaping.

Designing a Better System

With better incentives for reuse, stricter borewell tracking, and decentralized treatment, Bangalore can shift from a linear to a circular water economy. This story offers a snapshot of what that transformation must address.
